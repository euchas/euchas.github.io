About

Reading Mars for the story of its water.

I'm a planetary scientist and SNSF Postdoc.Mobility Fellow at ETH Zurich, joining the University of Cambridge as an incoming Fellow of the Leverhulme Centre for Life in the Universe. My research reconstructs how Mars evolved — its aqueous alteration, iron-oxide mineralogy, and the habitability of ancient crust.

PhD from Bern; degrees from Copenhagen and Vilnius; a fellowship at Brown. I work with ESA's CaSSIS, NASA's HiRISE, and the Perseverance rover — and, off the clock, host radio shows mixing electronic music with planetary science.

Research

Three threads, one planet.

01

Water & iron oxides

Ancient climate written into Martian dust — including ferrihydrite that records cold, wet conditions on early Mars.

02

Surface & volatiles

Transient frost on the Tharsis volcanoes, slope streaks, and seasonal change in high-resolution orbital colour.

03

Habitability & MSR

Deep crust and impact history at Jezero crater rim, and the case for Mars Sample Return.
