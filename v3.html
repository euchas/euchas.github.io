01 — About

A planetary scientist between the lab and the field.

I'm an SNSF Postdoc.Mobility Fellow at ETH Zurich, joining the University of Cambridge as an incoming Fellow of the Leverhulme Centre for Life in the Universe. My research reconstructs the geological and climatic evolution of Mars — its aqueous alteration, iron-oxide mineralogy, and the habitability of ancient crust.

PhD in Astronomy & Physics (Bern); MSc Geophysics (Copenhagen); BSc (Vilnius). I contribute to ESA's CaSSIS, NASA's HiRISE, and the Perseverance rover's Mastcam-Z, and I do analog fieldwork in Iceland and the Azores.

Off the clock, I host radio shows on NTS.live and RadioVilnius.live, mixing experimental electronic music with science — and once built an exhibition, Potentially Uninhabitable Worlds, with the artist Matteo Taramelli.
