About

I am a planetary scientist and SNSF Postdoc.Mobility Fellow at ETH Zurich, joining the University of Cambridge as an incoming Fellow of the Leverhulme Centre for Life in the Universe. My research asks how Mars evolved from a potentially habitable world into today's cold desert.

I earned my PhD in Astronomy & Physics at the University of Bern, with earlier degrees from Copenhagen and Vilnius. I've held research fellowships at Brown University and ETH Zurich, and I contribute to three flagship Mars missions: ESA's CaSSIS, NASA's HiRISE, and the Perseverance rover's Mastcam-Z.

I'm also a radio host and science communicator, blending experimental electronic music with planetary science on NTS.live and RadioVilnius.live.

02

Research

Combining orbital hyperspectral remote sensing, laboratory spectroscopy, machine learning on planetary datasets, and analog fieldwork in volcanic and hydrothermal terrains.

Water & iron oxides

Reading ancient climate from Martian dust mineralogy — including ferrihydrite that records cold, wet conditions on early Mars.

Surface processes

Transient frost on the Tharsis volcanoes, slope streaks, and seasonal change captured in high-resolution orbital colour.

Habitability & MSR

Deep crust and impact history at Jezero crater rim, and what they mean for the samples Perseverance is caching.

Nature Geoscience · Cover

Water frost on Martian mountaintops

My discovery of transient morning frost on the Tharsis volcanoes made the cover of Nature Geoscience (July 2024) and reshaped our picture of the modern Martian water cycle — with coverage from CNN, the BBC, and dozens more.
